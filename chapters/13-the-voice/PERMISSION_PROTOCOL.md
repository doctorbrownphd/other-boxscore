## Purpose

This document defines the process by which Chapter 13 obtains, records, and maintains permission to present oral history testimony. Every excerpt in the chapter requires documented permission. This document describes who makes contact, what they say, how responses are recorded, and what happens when permission is declined.

---

## 1. Outreach Process

### Who contacts

The project owner (Jeremy Haynes) initiates all permission outreach. No agent, contributor, or automated system contacts rights holders or institutions on behalf of the project without explicit authorization from the project owner.

For institutional collections, the initial contact is directed to the institution's rights and reproductions office, special collections librarian, or archivist, as appropriate to the institution's published contact structure.

For individual rights holders (estates, independent interviewers), the project owner makes direct contact.

### Outreach template

All outreach uses the following structure. The language may be adapted to fit the institution or individual, but all five elements must be present:

1. **Identification.** The project name (The Other Box Score), URL (theotherboxscore.org), and the project owner's name and contact information.
2. **Purpose.** A clear statement that the project is a non-commercial, open-source data journalism platform dedicated to the Negro Leagues, licensed under MIT (code) and CC0 (data).
3. **Specific request.** Identification of the specific recording(s) for which permission is sought, the nature of the use (transcript excerpt, audio excerpt, metadata indexing), and the maximum scope of the material to be presented.
4. **Attribution commitment.** A statement that the holding institution or rights holder will be credited at the point of every excerpt, with a link to the institution's own access point for the full recording.
5. **Refusal respect.** A statement that declined permissions are respected without follow-up, and that the recording will remain in the chapter's metadata index (title, speaker, institution, access pathway) but no excerpt material will be presented.

### Timeline

- Initial outreach is sent at least 90 days before the chapter's planned publication date.
- A single follow-up is sent 30 days after initial outreach if no response has been received.
- If no response is received after the follow-up, the recording is classified as "permission not obtained" and treated identically to a declined permission for publication purposes. The outreach record is preserved in the permission ledger for future re-contact if circumstances change.
- No third contact is made without a substantive change in circumstances (new institutional contact, new collection policy, or institutional outreach to the project).

## 2. Institution Types

### Open collections

Recordings in collections with published open-access or Creative Commons licenses. Permission outreach is still conducted to confirm the license applies to the specific recording and to the intended use (excerpt republication on a third-party platform). The license terms are recorded in the permission ledger.

### Restricted collections

Recordings in collections that require credentialed access (academic affiliation, on-site visit, research application). The chapter's archive index includes the recording's metadata and the institution's access requirements. Excerpt material is only included if the institution grants explicit permission for third-party presentation, which is separate from granting research access.

### By-request collections

Recordings in collections where access and reproduction permissions are evaluated per request. The outreach process described above applies in full. These collections typically have the longest response timelines and the most specific conditions on approved use. All conditions are recorded verbatim in the permission ledger.

## 3. Excerpt Criteria

### What qualifies as an excerpt

An excerpt is a continuous segment of a recording's transcript or audio, presented in the chapter with attribution. Excerpts serve one of the following editorial purposes:

- **Speaker introduction.** A passage in which the speaker describes their own background, entry into the Negro Leagues, or playing career. Used in the featured speaker sections and the archive explorer.
- **Topic illustration.** A passage in which the speaker addresses one of the chapter's indexed topics (barnstorming, segregation, economics, integration, etc.). Used in the topic map section.
- **Gap documentation.** A passage in which a speaker describes another player who is absent from the oral history record. Used in the gap analysis section, clearly labeled as secondary testimony.

### Maximum length

- Audio excerpts: 90 seconds maximum per excerpt. No more than three audio excerpts per recording.
- Transcript excerpts: 300 words maximum per excerpt. No more than five transcript excerpts per recording.
- These limits may be adjusted downward per the rights holder's conditions. They are never adjusted upward without the rights holder's explicit approval.

### Context requirements

Every excerpt is presented with:

- Speaker name and biographical identification
- Recording date (year at minimum, full date where known)
- Holding institution name and collection identifier
- Interviewer name where documented
- A brief contextual note (one to two sentences) identifying what the speaker is discussing, positioned after the excerpt per the speaker primacy standard in TESTIMONY_ETHICS.md
- Permission status confirmation (referencing the permission ledger entry)

## 4. Declined Permissions

When a rights holder or institution declines permission for excerpt use:

1. The decision is recorded in the permission ledger with the date and any stated reason (if the rights holder provides one; the platform does not request reasons).
2. All excerpt material from the recording is removed from the chapter within 48 hours of the decline.
3. The recording remains in the archive index with full metadata: title, speaker, date, holding institution, collection identifier, and access pathway. The index entry includes a note: "Excerpt material not available. Contact [institution name] for access to the full recording."
4. The recording's topic tags remain in the topic model if they were derived from metadata or publicly available descriptions. Topic tags derived from transcript content are removed.
5. No follow-up outreach is conducted regarding the declined recording unless the rights holder initiates contact.
6. If a declined permission affects one of the three featured speakers, the editorial team evaluates whether the featured speaker section can be maintained with the remaining permitted material. If not, the section is restructured to note the limitation and direct readers to the holding institution.

## 5. Living Speaker Protocol

For testimony from speakers who are living at the time of chapter publication:

1. **Direct contact required.** The project owner contacts the speaker directly, not solely through an institutional intermediary. If the speaker's testimony was collected by an institution, both the institution and the speaker are contacted.
2. **Informed consent.** The speaker receives a written description of the project, the specific excerpts proposed for inclusion, and the editorial context in which the excerpts will appear. The description is written in plain language, not legal or academic register.
3. **Review opportunity.** The speaker is offered the opportunity to review the final presentation of their testimony before publication. The review period is at least 14 days.
4. **Documented approval.** The speaker's approval is recorded in the permission ledger with the date, the scope of approved use, and any conditions. Email confirmation is acceptable. Verbal approval is documented in a contemporaneous written record by the project owner.
5. **Withdrawal right.** The speaker may withdraw approval at any time after publication. Withdrawal is processed within 48 hours per TESTIMONY_ETHICS.md Section 6.
6. **No promotional use.** The speaker is not contacted for endorsements, testimonials about the platform, or promotional appearances. The only contact is for permission, review, and any follow-up the speaker initiates.

## 6. Estate Protocol

For testimony from speakers who are deceased, rights may have passed to the speaker's estate, the interviewer, the holding institution, or some combination.

### Rights holder identification

1. The holding institution is contacted first to confirm whether the institution holds reproduction rights or whether rights reside with the speaker's estate or the interviewer.
2. If rights reside with the estate, the project owner identifies the estate's representative through public records, published obituaries, institutional referrals, or professional genealogical research services.
3. If the estate representative cannot be identified after documented effort, the recording is classified as "rights holder unidentified" in the permission ledger. No excerpt material is included. The recording remains in the archive index with full metadata and a note directing researchers to the holding institution.

### Outreach template for estates

Estate outreach follows the same five-element structure as institutional outreach (Section 1) with two additions:

- A statement acknowledging the speaker's contribution to the historical record and the platform's intent to honor that contribution.
- An explicit statement that the estate has full authority to decline, condition, or withdraw permission, and that the platform will comply without question.

### Timeline

Estate outreach follows the same 90-day / 30-day timeline as institutional outreach. Estate contacts often require longer response periods; the project owner exercises judgment on follow-up timing while adhering to the single-follow-up limit.

## 7. Permission Ledger

All permission records are maintained in `data/permission-ledger.json`. The ledger is the chapter's primary compliance artifact and is reviewed by the full agent roster at every build gate.

### Ledger schema (per recording)

```json
{
  "recording_id": "sabr-oneil-2000",
  "speaker": "Buck O'Neil",
  "institution": "SABR Oral History Collection",
  "collection_id": "SABR-OH",
  "rights_holder": "SABR",
  "rights_holder_type": "institution",
  "contact_date": "2026-03-01",
  "follow_up_date": null,
  "response_date": "2026-03-15",
  "permission_status": "granted",
  "permission_scope": "transcript excerpts, max 300 words per excerpt, max 5 excerpts",
  "conditions": "Attribution to SABR Oral History Collection required at point of use",
  "declined_reason": null,
  "living_speaker": false,
  "speaker_approval_date": null,
  "speaker_review_offered": null,
  "contact_person": "Jeremy Haynes",
  "notes": ""
}
```

### Permission status values

- `granted` -- Permission obtained with documented scope and conditions.
- `granted-conditional` -- Permission obtained with specific restrictions beyond the standard scope.
- `pending` -- Outreach sent, no response received, within the 90-day window.
- `follow-up-sent` -- Single follow-up sent, awaiting response.
- `not-obtained` -- No response received after follow-up period. Treated as declined for publication purposes.
- `declined` -- Rights holder explicitly declined.
- `withdrawn` -- Permission previously granted, subsequently withdrawn by rights holder or speaker.
- `rights-holder-unidentified` -- Estate or rights holder could not be identified after documented effort.

### Ledger maintenance

- The ledger is updated within 24 hours of any permission-related communication.
- The ledger is never backdated. All entries reflect the actual date of the recorded event.
- The ledger is version-controlled in the repository. Changes to the ledger are committed with the standard commit convention: `[13-the-voice] data: update permission ledger -- [brief description]`.
- The ledger does not contain personal contact information (email addresses, phone numbers, mailing addresses). Contact details are maintained in a non-public file outside the repository.
