Fifty men. Eight hundred and eighty-eight seasons.

Each card lists what is on record -- name, position, years active, one statistic that survived the gap -- and one number, in barrier red, that does not appear in any official book: the seasons each man spent excluded from the major leagues. Below each, in monospace, one line about what he did with that time anyway.

The sum, across these fifty names, of the years they spent at the top of their game and outside the official record. Roughly enough to staff every Negro Leagues franchise of 1947 for a full decade. A statistical mass with no place to land in the books that ever counted it.

The players below have no verified public domain photograph available. The photographic record of the Negro Leagues was not preserved with the same institutional care as the major leagues. That absence is part of what was lost.

The last line on Cool Papa Bell's card is not a statistic. It is what the statistics could not contain. That is intentional, and Bell's is not the only card on which it applies.
