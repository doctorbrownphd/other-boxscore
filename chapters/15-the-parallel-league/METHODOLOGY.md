## What This Chapter Does

This chapter is the platform's coda. It does not introduce new data, new models, or new analysis. It assembles the findings from the preceding fourteen chapters into a single argument, stated in plain language with precise citations to the chapters that produced each finding. The chapter presents five core findings: a $3.7 billion wage gap (Chapter 08), a maximum franchise integration delay of 12 years and 96 days (Chapter 09), Negro League players above Hall of Fame positional medians by rate JAWS (Chapters 10--11), a 67-year gap between Cuban Hall of Fame and Cooperstown induction for Jose Mendez (Chapter 12), and the Black press advocacy arc that built and then argued for the dissolution of the Negro Leagues (Chapter 14). Every number in this chapter traces to a prior chapter's methodology. This chapter's contribution is editorial synthesis, not original computation.

## Analytical Methods

This chapter applies no analytical methods. It is an editorial synthesis of prior findings. The only intellectual operation is selection (which findings to present) and arrangement (in what order). Both are editorial choices, not analytical methods.

### Editorial Selection Criteria

The five findings were selected to represent the platform's breadth: economic impact (Ch 08), institutional resistance to integration (Ch 09), statistical merit (Ch 10--11), international recognition patterns (Ch 12), and the journalism infrastructure that documented all of it (Ch 14). Each finding is the central or most striking result from its chapter.

### Box Score Construction (Fig 02)

The chapter includes a side-by-side box score from August 1, 1943: an American League game (Cleveland at Detroit) and the East-West All-Star Game at Comiskey Park. Both box scores are reconstructed from contemporary sources. The MLB game data is from Baseball Reference. The East-West All-Star Game data is from the Seamheads Negro Leagues Database and contemporary newspaper accounts. All player names, at-bats, runs, hits, and RBI values are Documented.

## AI-Generated Content

### Editorial Synthesis
- **Generated by:** Claude (Anthropic), used in editorial development
- **Prompt structure:** Chapter author provided the five selected findings with citations; Claude assisted with narrative structure, voice consistency, and editorial framing
- **Inputs to the prompt:** Prior chapter findings with confidence labels, selected oral history passages, box score data
- **Output:** Prose synthesis connecting findings into a single argument
- **Confidence label:** AI-assisted content is labeled in the chapter's design. All factual claims are cited to prior chapters.
- **Human review:** Every sentence was reviewed for factual accuracy against the cited chapter's data. No finding is restated at a higher confidence level than its source chapter assigns.
- **Accuracy standard:** Every number in the chapter traces to a prior chapter's documented output. No new factual claims are introduced.
- **Known limitations:** The editorial framing (what to emphasize, what order, what language) is an interpretive act. The chapter presents one reading of the platform's findings. Other framings are possible.

### Oral History Passages (Section 3)
- **Content type:** Five testimony passages attributed to Buck O'Neil, Mamie "Peanut" Johnson, Wendell Smith, Cool Papa Bell, and Monte Irvin
- **Source:** Chapter 13 (The Voice) oral history and archival materials
- **Confidence label:** Each quote is attributed to the speaker with source context (oral history, interview, archive). Exact wording and attribution verified against Chapter 13 documentation.
- **Known limitations:** Oral history quotations are drawn from transcriptions. Minor variations in wording may exist between sources.

## Disputed Claims

| Claim | Dispute or uncertainty | Sources consulted | How presented in chapter |
|-------|----------------------|-------------------|--------------------------|
| $3.7 billion wage gap | The confidence interval is wide. The floor estimate exceeds $2 billion but the central estimate carries documented uncertainty. | Chapter 08 methodology | Presented with both the central estimate and the floor of the confidence interval. |
| Negro League players "above the threshold" | The rate-based JAWS methodology produces rankings sensitive to incomplete season data and era adjustment assumptions. | Chapters 10--11 methodologies | Presented as "measurements from the now-official statistical record," noting they are based on the Seamheads data incorporated into the official MLB record. |
| "The record is official now" | MLB's 2020 decision to recognize Negro League statistics as part of the official record is the basis for this claim. The completeness and integration of that recognition is ongoing. | MLB announcement (December 2020), subsequent record updates | Stated as institutional fact. The closing line of the chapter. |
