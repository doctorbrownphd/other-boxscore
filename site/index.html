Hey --

Have you heard
of Pumpsie Green?

Infielder Red Sox . 1959 Team #16 of 16 4,480 days late

He was the last man. July 21, 1959.

The Boston Red Sox became the final major league team to integrate -- twelve years, three months, and six days after Jackie Robinson. Pumpsie Green entered as a pinch runner. Tom Yawkey had owned the team the entire time.

Have you heard
of Cool Papa Bell?

CF . 1922--1946 .325 career avg 175+ SB in one season HOF 1974

He could flip the light and be in bed before the room went dark.

James "Cool Papa" Bell stole over 175 bases in a single season. He spent 29 years in the Negro Leagues. He never played a major league game. He made the Hall of Fame in 1974 -- on the testimony of Satchel Paige, who said Bell was the fastest man alive.

Have you heard
of Leon Day?

RHP . 1934--1946 7 All-Star games D-Day veteran HOF 1995

He pitched on the morning of D-Day.

June 6, 1944, in a military game in England. He came home and threw an Opening Day no-hitter in 1946. He never started a major league game. He made the Hall of Fame in 1995 -- one year before he died.

Have you heard
of Cristobal Torriente?

OF . 1913--1932 .333 career avg Outhit Ruth in Cuba HOF 2006

He outhit Babe Ruth in Havana, 1920.

In a barnstorming series that October, Ruth batted .345. Torriente batted .433. The mainstream American press did not file a story. He is now third all-time in career batting average. He always was.

Have you heard
of Biz Mackey?

C . 1920--1947 .322 career avg Taught Campanella HOF 2006

He taught Roy Campanella to catch.

Campanella went to the Hall of Fame in 1969. Mackey waited until 2006 -- sixteen years after he died. He was the finest defensive catcher of his generation. His generation was not allowed to play in the major leagues.

Have you heard
of Frank Grant?

2B . 1886--1903 .337 in white minors Last man before the ban HOF 2006

He was the last one in before the door closed.

The last Black player in white organized baseball before the gentleman's agreement locked him out -- 1903. He batted .337 in the minors when they still let him play. Then they stopped letting him. He never got back in.

About this project

The other box score
was always there.

For fifty years, the best baseball players in the world played in leagues the official record refused to count. The Pittsburgh Courier covered every game. The crowds came. The statistics accumulated.

Nobody was writing them down in a book that counted.

This platform is that book. Fifteen chapters and a signature interactive feature, organized as five parts, a coda, and a lineup builder. Each one answers the same question from a different direction: why don't you know their names?

Everything here is open. The data, the code, the methodology. MIT license on the software. CC0 on the data. Free forever.
